FSBO Deal Tracker for Fix-and-Flip Investors

Fix-and-flip buyers should not deep-comp every lead. The better workflow is to screen aggressively first, then reserve full comp work and contractor review for the finalists.

The problem

Most flip buyers burn time on the wrong addresses. They comp too early, before they know whether the seller is motivated, the asking price is softening, or the photos show real deferred maintenance.

What the product helps you do

  • Use photo condition AI to spot listing sets that show clutter, dated finishes, damage, or obvious neglect.
  • Combine price-to-value gap, days on market, and price cuts to identify owners whose expectations may be moving.
  • Keep a shortlist of “screened-in” addresses before you do the heavier comp and rehab work.

How investors use it in practice

  • Filter for higher-score listings in your target price band.
  • Open only the top tier of candidates for detailed deal math and offer modeling.
  • Use the Advisor to run quick ARV, margin, and rule-of-thumb checks before investing more time.

Hard limit

  • This does not replace local comp work, contractor bids, inspection, or permit review.
  • The goal is to get you “close enough to investigate,” not to tell you to skip underwriting.

Typical workflow

01

Screen

Use score, photos, price movement, and filters to narrow the field quickly.

02

Investigate

Open only the shortlisted listings for contact, notes, Advisor prompts, and deeper math.

03

Decide

Move finalists into real comps, local due diligence, and your actual offer process.
